The brief

For London based home owners/ first-time buyers, who want to improve their living space 1roof.com is an online service that allows people to get matched with relevant tradesmen and find inspiration. This service is reliable and hassle-free. The product offers an end to end tailored guidance.

The outcome

UX Research

Get to know your users first and find out about their needs and goals.

Competitve Analysis

We analysed a number of similar online providers offering similar services to 1ROOF.Majority of the UK based competitors serve as directories of Pros offering their services but there were very few that on top of that would offer guidance, ideas and inspiration to their users. It became obvious that there is a gap on the market where users need both, i.e. to be able to quickly find a relevant Pro for their job but also to see how similar projects were succesfully completed in the past and draw inspiration from those examples.

User survey

The purpose of the survey was to provide input for the design process and gather quantitative data from participants. Two key takeaway from the survey results was that there is a need and want to people to use online services to hire contractors and online was their prefrerred channel. However, there was a significant trust issue that we needed to address.

User Interviews

While the survey provided us with quantitave data, in order to further inform our design process we needed qualitative data and the answer as to why users were worried to buy or sell custom-made products online. We interviewed 5 users and the information gained during this process helped us to develop our personas.

Personas

Based on the data and information gathered during earlier reserach we created our primary personas, i.e. representatives of the target audience segments whose needs and goals we needed to take into account while designing the solution. We established that there were two primary user groups. First group were those users who knew what type of service they needed and wanted to quickly and easily find the most relevant Pro for their job. Second group, on the other hand, was lookig more for inspiration and proof of previous project successes.

Design Process

Make your goals clear and defined and validate your ideas early on!

Design studio & sketches

We started off design process by conducting a Design studio with 1ROOF, during which we brainstormed and sketched ideas for the problesm identified duirng reserach phase. We tested those ideas by asking the client themselves and users to complete a task using a paper prototype we created from sketches. We then iterated on the skteches based on feedback received during testing sessions.

Wireframes

Following testing and iterations, once we were happy with the paper prototype, we proceeded onto digital wireframes. Considering our two different personas we needed to design two user journeys, i.e. a Find a Pro form and Browse projects for inspiration and ideas.

Testing with Users

The only way to validate your design ideas is to test them with real users.

Usability testing

We tested our wireframes several times with users and the client alike. The insights and feedback gathered during testing sessions led to further iterations on the wireframes until when users and 1ROOF could complete tasks given to them during usability testing without any major issues.

Prototype

From wireframes to visuals and how to create a MVP.

The Prototype

Next and last stage in the process was to develop visual design for our two new user journeys and then to create an interactive prototype using high-fidelity mock-ups. Having done user reserach upfront and several rounds of testing with users throughout the process allowed us to catch any issues or problems early on so building the final visual prototype was straightforward and stress free.
