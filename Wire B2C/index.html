UX Research

Learn about the business and understand the needs of your customers.

Competitive Analysis

I looked at a number of sites offering similar products and services, in the UK, US and Australia. Majority of these sites were very much product focused with also some offering e-commerce solutions. There were only a few sites that had more comprehensive case studies showcasing the scope of service provided or how a particular product or solution was fit for a problem. Finding the products was mainly via traditional navigation or search. Some competitors also had a map of their branches or depots that appeared to be of value to the customers as we found it later during customer interviews.


Stakeholder interviews

The business already had a solid customer base who were used to and liked doing business face to face and which customer base the business needed to retain. However, in order to stay competitive on the increasingly changing market, Mabey needed to win new and more customers, i.e. those who were more technology focused and who used the online services as their first resort.

What we also learnt during this stage was that the existing Mabey customers varied largely and while some of them were mainly interested in technical specification of the products or locality of the services, others might have been interested in the performance of the business and the history of projects completed around the world.

Customer interviews

The next stage of the project was to talk to a number of Mabey customers to find out how they were using Mabey services and establish of there were any pain-points in their current customer journeys. While the survey provided us with quantitative data, in order to further inform our design process we needed qualitative data and the answers to the Why's already established by the user survey. To this end, we interviewed 5 users. These interviews allowed us to get a deeper understanding of the users who will use the feature we were to design.


Personas

I used the data and information gathered from the user research to create Mabey personas. I order to do that I analysed the information provided by the stakeholder and the customers we interviewed and created affinity maps for goals, needs and issues by extracting this information from the interviews we carried out. To add more depth and personality to each persona I provided a description based on their work tasks, habits and tools used. This has led us to three different personas.



User journeys and user flows

The next stage in the process was to map out the user journeys for the audience types we have created and discover the areas where they are most likely to experience issues in the current process. User journey mapping allowed us to identify the pain points that we needed to address for the project to be successful. Aside from the user journeys I also created task flows for key tasks identified for each persona to establish what activities and features are they likely to go through in order to perform their tasks successfully. This was an important step that proved to be very helpful when designing different parts of the new platform.




Design Process

Start with pen and paper to test and validate your ideas before moving onto the wireframes.

Sketches

I started sketching from the "most detailed" page, i.e. a product page and a project/ case study page. By doing so I was able to then incorporate the elements of the information into the mapper and product finder. While some products/ services were for both sale and hire, having done several iterations on paper I came up with the solution of catering for both cases before moving onto the wireframes.

Wireframes

Each sketch was followed by a wireframe. Releasing the wireframes for client's feedback in stages allowed us to further iterate on the designs as the project progressed. This also ensured that I was able to review the flow each time a new wireframe was produced and make any necessary changes.
