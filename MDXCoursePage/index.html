The outcome

A new design of the course page that takes into account the issues identified duirng usability testing and is more student-centred - thus allowing prospective students to relate to the current students co-hort and to make a well informed decision with regards to their choice of course.

UX Research

Get to know your users first and find out about their needs.

Competitve Analysis

The first step undertaken as part of this project covered a review of other university sites with particular focus on course pages. My review included Leeds beckett University, Leeds Beckett, Queens University in Belfast and a US based univeristy Bucknell. There were some clear patterns I identfied incl. horizontal on-page navigation that becomes sticky once yser tsrats scrolling down the page as well as persistent CTAs for applying for the course or visitng open days. While Bucknell Univeristy's unique features including personalisation of content and "Visited pages" seemed very useful for uders, the course page wasn't as engaging as that of Beckett or UEL.

Review of findings from usability testing of the current site

The client had commissioned usability testing on the site and provided us with te results. Having access to the usability report allowed me to identofy the main issues found with the course page which were my main focus when working on the new design. The most sever all of issues found was the fact that the key course content and CTAs were placedin the right hand side which most people tend to ignore seeing as "additional" but not primary content.

Design Process

Start with pen and paper to test and validate your ideas before moving onto the wireframes.

Sketches

I started the design process by sketching out the layout of the new course page focusing on how best to communicate the key course information, on the main and secondary CTAs and the navigation which was essential in helping the user to navigate between the different sections of the course page. I tried two approaches with vertical and horizontal navigation and considering a significant number of sections on the page I felt vertical (sticky) navigation would be more appropriate as it is more scalable and likely remains more visible to the user as they navigate between difefrent sections on the page. For mobile I chose a dropdown for the navigation that also remain attached to the top of the screen giving the user access the option to access it at any time.


Wireframes

Once I was happy with the sketches and the different section elements and their interactions, next stage in the process was to create the wirefarme for the course page.
