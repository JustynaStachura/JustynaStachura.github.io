1ROOF.
The right people for your home.

Overview

1ROOF was of the group rojects I completed as part of the UX course with General Assembly.

Below is a snapshot of the problem, the tools and techniques we used during the design process.

The brief

For London based home owners/ first-time buyers, who want to improve their living space 1roof.com is an online service that allows people to get matched with relevant tradesmen and find inspiration. This service is reliable and hassle-free. The product offers an end to end tailored guidance.

UX Research

Get to know your users first and find out about their needs.

Competitve Analysis

We analysed a number of similar services with services like Folksy, Bespokee or Custom Made being main online competitors.We discovered that while both Etsy and thier competitors were alreday offerring custom-made service to their customers in some form the solution offered wasn't always the most staright-forward or user-friendly.

User survey

The purpose of the survey was to provide input for the design process and gather quantitative data from participants. Two mkey takeaways from the survey were that buyesr did not buy custom-made products as they were worried they wouldn't come out the way they wanted, and the sellers mentioned logistical challenges around the process.

User Interviews

While the survey provided us with quantitave data, in order to further inform our design process we needed qualitative data and the answer as to why users were worried to buy or sell custom-made products online. We interviewed 5 users and the information gained during this process helped us to develop our personas.

Personas

From the data and information gathered during the reserach we created our primary personas, i.e. a buyer and a seller. Personas are representatives of particular audeince segments whose needs and pain points we needed to address.

Testing with Users

The only way to validate your design ideas is to test them with real users.

Usability testing

We tested our wireframes several times with users. The insights and feedback gathered during those sessions led to further iterations on wireframes until when we were confident that the users can complete their tasks without any major issues.
