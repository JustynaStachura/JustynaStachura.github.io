Travelex Money Card.
The safer way to spend abroad.

Overview

Project in a snapshot:

the problem, the tools and techniques used during the design process and the outcome.

The need

Poor online experience for prepaid cards due to bad signposting and disjointed MasterCard integration. Refresh of the Travelex prepaid card product, improvement of signposting for card registration, login and card top up as the interim solution for card management portal.

Tools and methods used

Trustpilot review analysisbr /> Competitive analysis
Stakeholder workshop
Customer journey mapping
User flows
Wireframes
UI

Outcome

New product identity for Travelex prepaid card, improved signposting for easier navigation and better customer journey.

UX Research

Understand the problem and learn about the needs of your customers.

Trustpilot reviews

Apart from talking to Customer Service Team I also analysed customer reviews on Trustpilot pertaining to their experience with Travelex Multicurrency Passport which prceeded Travelex Money Card. This gave me further insight into the problems and issues the customers were facing. While we weren't able to rectify everything starightaway there was a lot pf issuesd that we could address as part of the card refresh.

Competitors analysis

I looked at a number of competitors with similar product offering, primarily in the UK. While some of those products were similar to ours,, others were created by challenger banks and targeted young, digital savvy customers. Their customer journey was seamless across the different touchpoints and customer experience was exceptional. At Travelex, however, we were dealing with some technical legacy and hence needed to find a way to address the pain paints working with the limitations that were in place.

Going through this process helped me understand the steps in the customwr journey and identify the pain points such as the confusion around where to login and where the managed and top up the card.

Stakeholder workshop

I ran a workshop with internal stakeholders to better understand the existing user journey and find out about any organisational or commercial challenges around the product. I used a whiteboard to visualise the online journey and discover all the points where the customers needs were not being addressed adding to their disappointmnet and frustrations. I divided the journey into 4 stages:

1. Buying the card

2. Receiving the card and activating the card

3. Activate cars and register an account

4. Manage the card (with login)

Customer journey mapping

I used the data and information gathered from the user research to create Mabey personas. I order to do that I analysed the information provided by the stakeholder and the customers we interviewed and created affinity maps for goals, needs and issues by extracting this information from the interviews we carried out. To add more depth and personality to each persona I provided a description based on their work tasks, habits and tools used. This has led us to three different personas.

User flows

Next step in the process was exploring different user journeys in order to test and establish the best way to signpost the ways for customers to easily find their way to buy and regsiter the card, and to manage it and reload. In addition I also looked at the existing customers who already had one of the Travelex prepaid cards and allow them to explore and switch to the new product. One way of doing so was to create a landing page for all remaining products and from there direct customers to the new Travelex Money Card page.



Design Process

Start with pen and paper to test and validate your ideas before moving onto the wireframes.

Wireframes

Following UX research phase, having gained understanding of the key issues in the customer journey were and knowing the business objectives related to the release of the new product allowed me to move onto wireframes for the landing page for the new prepaid card. I did a few explorations trying different ways of positioning the key CTAs and different patterns for displaying the product walk through.

UI

Last stage in the process was applying the UI to the wirerames sogned off by the business. At the last minute we also added the currency widget at the request of the marketing team. I worked with a brand designer who developed new branding for the new product collateral. Although in contrast to the rest of Travelex website, we decided to go for a new, more contemporary look and feel for the new product page to bring ot closer to other similar products alreday on the market.

Final product

New pre-paid Travelex Money Card.

The final product

Moving onto the visual design, I worked closely with the visual designer during which process we had the opportunity to further improve the designs and test and review interactions once the site was in development. Although we could not implement everything as intended due to a tight deadline the client has engaged us in phase 2 of the project during which we will be looking to improve on the existing features and functionalities.
