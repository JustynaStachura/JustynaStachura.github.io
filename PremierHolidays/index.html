Premier Holidays.
Strategy for a perfect holiday

Overview

Project in a snapshot:

the problem, the tools and techniques we used during the UX process and the solution we came up with.

The brief

Come up with a strategy for businnes and design concepts for a new Premier Holidays site.

Customer Interviews
Personas

The outcome

Digital and business strategy that will allow Premier Holidays to move confidently into the digital space of the travel industry.

UX Research

Get to know your users first and find out about their needs.

Competitve Analysis

The process of market analysis was focused primarily on UK-based Premier Holidays' competitors. We looked at and analysed business such as Kuoni, Audley Travel, Black Tomato, Lux Tripper and Secret Escapes. Majority of them had alreday a well established web presence offering tailor-made, luxury or package holidays. We also noticed a strong trend for personalisation with features such as Bucket list and itinerary builder offering customers a holiday experience on their terms. Majority of key players in the online travel industry allowed customers to filter various packages and offers by a number of options which helped reduce the time spent on reserach and browsing.



Stakeholder interviews

We spoke to a number of business stakeholders incl. CEO as well as Bookings and IT team. It became clear that while the business was excellent at providing superb customer servive and high-end bespoke itineraries to its long-term customers, it was failing at reaching out directly to new audiences. One of the biggest issues recognised was the fact that over 80% of business was done via partners and travel agents instead of directly with customers. Most of the operations were carried out in an offline form and there was also a need for transitioning the business from analogue into digital one and establishing new online presence that would help to attract new customers and grow the revenue.

Customer interviews

Next step in the process was talking to Premier Holidays" customers. We interviewed several customers which allowed us to find out more about the process they went through when looking for and booking a holiday, what were their needs during this process and what issues they encountered on the way.

Personas

After collating and analysing the data from the customer interviews we looked at common patterns in terms of behaviour, goals and needs we established that our main customer groups were a deal seeker, an informed holiday maker and a bespoke customer.





User journeys & user flows

To further inform our design decisions we also created a user journey, a user flow and a mind map for each persona. These artefacts helped us to get into the shoes of our personas, discover what they think about when planning a holiday, how they feel and what difficulties or problems do they experience. Based on this we were able to prpvide recommendations for improvement and apply the findings during the design process later on.




Design Process

Make your goals clear and defined and validate your ideas early on!

Sketches

We started off design process by conducting a Design studio, during which we brainstormed and sketched ideas for the problesm identified duirng reserach phase. We tested those ideas by asking users to complete a task using a paper prototype we created from sketches. We then iterated on the skteches based on feedback received from users at this stage.

Wireframes

Once we were happy with the paper prototype, we proceeded onto digital wireframes. Considering our two different personas we needed to develop two flows: one for buyers and one for sellers. The UX research showed that while buyers were more likely to use a mobile or a tablet, sellers were mostly using laptops. Hence the two flows we developed were based on two different device types to reflect this.
